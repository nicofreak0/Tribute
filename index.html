Grace Brewster Murray Hopper

Born: December 9th, 1906 Died: January 1st, 1992

Summary

Grace Hopper was one of the first three modern programmers. She was a computer pioneer and naval officer. Grace invented COBOL, which stands for Common Business Oriented Language, one of the first programming languages invented. She is considered "the first hacker in history."

Innovating Brilliant Inspiring

Grace Hopper's Life.

  • Grace's father owned an insurance company and sent her to private schools.
  • She graduated Phi Beta Kappa in 1928 from Vassar College, with degrees in mathematics and physics.
  • In 1930 she received her master's degree in mathematics from Yale, and proceeded to start teaching mathematics in 1931, while pursuing her doctorate at Yale.
  • She earned her Ph.D. in mathematics and mathematical physics from Yale in 1934.
  • After the bombing of Pearl Harbor and the U.S.A's entry into WWII, Grace decided to join the army. Although she was originally rejected due to her age and size, she was able to join the U.S. Naval Reserve in december of 1943.
  • In 1946 she left active service in response to them turning down her request for regular commission due to her age.
  • Grace joined the Eckert-Mauchly Computer Corporation in Philadelphia as senior mathematician in 1949.
  • She became head programmer for Remington Rand as she worked on UNIVAC I (Universal Cutmatic Computer
  • In 1952 her programing team would develope the first computer language "compiler" called A-0.
  • In 1959 she helped invent and develope, what she is most known for, COBOL the first standardized general business computer language.
